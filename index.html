🕉 Introduction

The period between the 8th and 13th centuries was a very important time in India's social and cultural history. Great saints and thinkers like Shankaracharya, Ramanujacharya, Madhwacharya, and Basaveshwara lived during this time.

They helped people understand God and spirituality in simple ways. They all opposed caste and gender discrimination and taught people to surrender to God through the Bhakti Path (path of devotion).

🔹 Shankaracharya and Advaita Philosophy

Born in: Kaladi village, Kerala

Philosophy: Advaita (Non-dualism)

Main Idea: "Only Brahma is Truth; the world is false (illusion or Maya)"

Famous Quote: "Aham Brahmasmi" – I am Brahma

Family Background

  • Father: Shivaguru
  • Mother: Aryamba
  • Born in Namboodadi Brahmin family
  • Very intelligent - mastered all scriptures by age 7

Key Teachings

  • Brahma has no form, features, or qualities (Nirguna, Nirakara, Nirvikara)
  • The world is Maya (illusion)
  • Soul (Atma) and God (Brahma) are one
  • Knowledge is the path to Moksha

Important Books

  • Bhaja Govindam
  • Vivekachoodamani
  • Shivanandalahari
  • Shankarabhasya
  • Soundaryalahari

Peethas Established

  • Badari - Jyotir Peetha
  • Dwaraka - Kalika Peetha
  • Puri - Govardhan Peetha
  • Sringeri - Sharada Peetha
  • Kanchi - Kamakoti Peetha

🔹 Ramanujacharya and Vishishtadvaita Philosophy

Born in: Sriperumbudur, near Chennai

Philosophy: Vishishtadvaita (Qualified Non-dualism)

Main Idea: The world and soul are different from God, but depend on Him

Path to Moksha: Through Bhakti (devotion) and Prapathi (surrender)

Family Background

  • Father: Keshava Somayaji
  • Mother: Kanthimathi
  • Studied at Kanchi under Yadavaprakash
  • Became head of mutt at Srirangam

Key Teachings

  • Jeeva (soul) and Prakruthi (nature) are real
  • World is real form of Brahma, not illusion
  • Vishnu is the supreme God
  • Opposed caste system

Important Books

  • Geetha Bhashya
  • Vedanta Sangraha
  • Vedantasara
  • Vedanta Deepika
  • Sribhashya

Famous Mutts

  • Yatiraja Mutt - Melukote
  • Parakala Mutt - Mysuru
  • Ahobala Mutt - Guntur
  • Aandavan Mutt - Srirangam
  • Vamaimalai Mutt - Suchindram

🔹 Madhwacharya and Dvaita Philosophy

Born in: Pajaka village, Udupi, Karnataka

Philosophy: Dvaita (Dualism)

Main Idea: God and soul are different - God is master, human is servant

Belief: The world is real, not an illusion

Family Background

  • Father: Madhwagehabhatta
  • Mother: Vedavati
  • Studied under Achyutapreksha
  • Lord Vishnu was family deity

Key Teachings

  • Divine Soul and human soul are separate
  • Vishnu/Narayana is supreme God
  • World is real, not Maya
  • Worship of Vishnu leads to moksha

Important Books

  • Geetabhashya
  • Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya
  • Vishnutatparya Nirnaya
  • Bhagavata Tatparya Nirnaya
  • Mayavadakhandana

Ashta Mutts (8 Mutts)

  • Palimaru
  • Adamaru
  • Krishnapura
  • Puttige
  • Shirur
  • Sode
  • Kaniyoor
  • Pejawar

🔹 Basaveshwara and Veerashaiva Philosophy

Born in: Bagewadi, Vijayapura (Karnataka)

Movement: Social reform against caste and gender discrimination

Key Idea: "Work is Worship" - No job is big or small

Center: Anubhava Mantapa in Basava Kalyana

Family Background

  • Father: Madarasa
  • Mother: Madalambike
  • Rejected caste rituals from young age
  • Went to Kudalasangama for spiritual practice

Key Teachings

  • Every job is equal - Dignity of Labour
  • Body is the temple
  • God lives in everyone
  • No one is born untouchable
  • Purity of heart leads to salvation

Anubhava Mantapa

  • Spiritual center at Basava Kalyana
  • Discussed caste system issues
  • Addressed gender discrimination
  • Promoted truth, equality, devotion

Important Vachanakaras

  • Allama Prabhu
  • Akka Mahadevi
  • Siddarama
  • Ambigara Chowdaiah
  • Madivala Machayya
  • Madara Chennaiah

🧾 Conclusion

These four great thinkers – Shankaracharya, Ramanujacharya, Madhwacharya, and Basaveshwara – gave India different ways of understanding God, society, and life.

Though their philosophies were different, they all believed in equality, opposed discrimination, and wanted people to live truthfully and spiritually.

Their messages still guide people even today!
